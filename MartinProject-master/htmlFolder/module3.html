Consider These Questions While Reading The Documents and Watching The Videos

  • The life of nuclear plants has been extended in the US to 60,80 years; how can Japan learn from that experience; By extending reactor life and upgrades, nuclear power plants are getting better with age.
  • Contrast the roles of the NRA and NRC. How can regulation be both safe and streamlined?
  • Japan and the US are struggling with waste disposal? What are the options, including recycling/reprocessing; what are options for long term disposal of waste—“once through” as well as vitrified?
  • What are the investment needs for new build reactors? How many new reactors will the US and Japan need in the future to ensure energy security and decarbonization in line with climate objectives?
  • Nuclear Power is recovering in Japan; and is being enthusiastically promoted in the US. How can our nations work together in partnership to build new capacity?
  • Innovation is a key word among the younger generation of engineers? What innovations are possible in new power development regarding large scale and SMRs fission reactors as well as fusion?
  • How can the US and Japan work together to build capacity in other nations, especially developing nations to ensure safety and non-proliferation objectives? How will democratic countries compete with authoritative communist countries such as China and Russia to build infrastructure globally?
