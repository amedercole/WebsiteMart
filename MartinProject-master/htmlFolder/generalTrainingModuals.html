To learn from real-life stories and lessons shared by individuals involved in the Three Mile Island Unit 2 (TMI-2) accident that occurred approximately 40 years before the Fukushima Daiichi accident, as well as the subsequent response, to contemplate the decommissioning measures for Fukushima Daiichi and the reconstruction of Fukushima in the future.

US-Japan nuclear cooperation agreement
日米原子力協定

To understand nuclear power not only from a technological perspective but also from a policy standpoint, it is helpful to acknowledge the situation where termination of the agreement is possible with a six-month notice, and to comprehend the objectives for which the United States has entered into the agreement and the concerns of the United States. In particular, amidst the dominance of Russia and China in the global nuclear market, it is important to recognize the current situation where the nuclear non-proliferation regime built primarily by Japan, the United States, and Europe is being threatened. Through the nuclear agreement, it is reaffirmed that Japan's nuclear technology plays an essential role in global stability.
6カ月前通知により当該協定の終了が可能と規定 されている状況を再認識するとともに、米国が 当該協定を締結している目的および米国の懸念 事項を理解することにより、原子力を技術面の みでなく政策面からも理解することの一助とす る。 特に、ロシア・中国が世界の原子力市場を席巻 する状況下、日米欧を中心に築いてきた核不拡 散体制が脅かされている現状を認識するととも に、日本の原子力技術が世界の安定化に不可欠 な役割を果たしていることを、原子力協定を通 じて再確認する。

FAQ

The US-Japan Energy Institute will focus on three main objectives

  1. Identify key topics for online training that focus on problem solving in the real world today
  2. Select key distinguished speakers from Japan and the United States that will offer short videos and papers (all translated into Japanese and English)
  3. Compile a history of US-Japan collaboration over a fifty year period — 1973-2023, highlighting important global and national events which have impacted national economies and national security
  4. Linkages within the website will allow easy access between the training modules, historical event and key speakers including a large library of publications in Japanese and English. While the website will be complex and comprehensive and serve as an archive for research purposes the training courses themselves will be easily accessible in modules which will take a trainee no more than one hour a week.

A full introductory course will include 11 modules — so the entire course can be taken in only 11 hours. Videos will be no longer than five minutes and articles can be read in less than 15 minutes. The aim is to take complex history and experience and make it discernible and practical to trainees (young and old) that need to have an accurate perspective of current US-Japan energy issues today.
