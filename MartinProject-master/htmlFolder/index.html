Learn About The History of Nuclear Energy

The course will include special attention to nuclear energy, including its history and potential. A special lecture will be given by Susan Eisenhower about her grandfather’s vision “Atoms for Peace.” Experts will look at the potential for new reactors and designs, as well as the potential to extend the life of reactors beyond their 40 year life.

Nuclear energy offers important benefits to energy security and decarbonization, but it must be handled safely. Finally, the course will examine investment strategies for nuclear energy and the role of private-government partnership.

Learn From World Class Lecturers

The faculty for the training course are well known experts of energy policy, nuclear energy and climate policy in the US and Japan. They share a common interest to see nuclear energy revive to achieve energy security and environmental objectives.

International speakers such as the head of IEA and the Nuclear Energy Agency will be included. All have one thing in common — they believe that US-Japan relations form the key of a better, cleaner and safer world. They include company CEOs, distinguished regulators, former high level officials from Japan and the United States and well known think tanks in Japan and the United States such as IEEJ.

We even have a special audio interview between Prime Minister Nakasone and William Martin where the Prime Minister talks about the importance of energy security, nuclear energy and the US-Japan Nuclear Agreement.

About Us

The US-Japan Energy Institute recognizes that the US and Japan represent the most important bilateral relationship in the world and the partnership is essential for global peace, prosperity, energy security and decarbonization. The Institute includes a history of US Japan relations (1973-2023) including distinguished lecturers from both the US and Japan. A focus is on decarbonization, including the importance of nuclear power and renewables.

The Center is also dedicated to promoting Electricity for Humanity The Center was founded by William F. Martin, former Deputy Secretary of Energy who was awarded the Order of the Rising Sun by Emperor Akihito in 2018.
